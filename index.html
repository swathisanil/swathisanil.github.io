Customer Segment

A UK online retailer needed to understand why revenue growth had plateaued despite high transaction volumes. Leadership lacked visibility into which customers were driving lifetime value and which product categories were underperforming.

Extracted and cleaned 500K+ transactions in MySQL. Built RFM segmentation in Python (Pandas) to classify customers by Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value. Conducted cohort analysis to track retention across acquisition months. Forecasted 6-month revenue using time-series modelling.

Delivered an interactive Power BI dashboard with DAX-driven KPIs: monthly revenue trends, customer tier breakdown, product category contribution, and churn risk flagging — enabling the team to act on insight without running a single query.

Python MySQL Power BI DAX RFM Segmentation Cohort Analysis Time Series
View on GitHub ↗ Live Dashboard ↗
Key Findings
01
The top 20% of customers generated 68% of total revenue — yet had no loyalty mechanism in place to retain them.
02
Q4 cohorts showed 40% drop-off within 90 days, indicating a post-holiday churn problem that wasn't visible in aggregate monthly reporting.
03
Three product categories accounted for 82% of repeat purchases — but received the same marketing spend as underperforming lines.
04
RFM scoring identified a dormant high-value segment (high historical spend, no recent orders) — a win-back opportunity worth an estimated £180K in reactivation revenue.
Business Recommendations
Prioritise a loyalty programme for the top 20% customer tier. Reallocate 30% of marketing budget toward the three high-repeat categories. Launch a targeted win-back campaign for the dormant RFM segment with personalised incentives timed 75–90 days post-purchase.
